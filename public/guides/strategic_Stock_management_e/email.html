The Strategic Stock Management tab allows users to manage products that require special stockholding treatment outside the normal replenishment proposal workflow.
 
This feature is designed for exception-based inventory decisions where a product must be handled differently due to customer requirements, business risk, lifecycle stage, operational importance, or temporary supply considerations.
 
 
Proposal Workflow Restriction
 
When a product has a Strategic Stock Type, proposal workflow actions in the Proposal Report are disabled for that product.
 
This is intentional. Strategic stock products are treated as exceptions and are not meant to be processed through the normal accept/apply proposal workflow.

Where to Find It
 
 
To access this feature:
 
1. Open the Products menu in Odoo.
2. Select the required product.
3. Open the Strategic Stock Management tab.
 
This tab is available on the product form and is managed individually for each product.
 
Purpose of Strategic Stock Management
 
Strategic Stock Management is used when a product needs temporary or special stockholding rules that should not be driven only by the standard inventory proposal calculations.
 
Because these scenarios are product-specific, they are maintained directly on the product record rather than in the general settings page.
 
How Strategic Stock Management Works
 
Standard Flow
 
The typical process is:
 
1. Open the product.
2. Go to the Strategic Stock Management tab.
3. Select a Strategic Stock Type.
4. Enter the request details.
5. Enter the relevant customer details if needed.
6. Define the stock period and review dates.
7. Enter the target New Min and New Max values.
8. Save the product.
 
Once saved, the strategic stock data is recorded against the product, and the target reorder values are synced to the product’s reordering rule.
 
Automatic Behavior
 
Reordering Rule Synchronization
 
When New Min and New Max are entered and saved, the module updates the product’s reordering rule accordingly.
 
If no reordering rule exists, the module can create one for the product in the company warehouse context.
 
Proposal Workflow Restriction
 
When a product has a Strategic Stock Type, proposal workflow actions in the Proposal Report are disabled for that product.
 
This is intentional. Strategic stock products are treated as exceptions and are not meant to be processed through the normal accept/apply proposal workflow.
 
Automatic Reversion After End Date
 
The module includes a scheduled daily process that checks for expired strategic stock records.
 
When the strategic stock end date has passed, the system reverts the reordering rule.
 
In general:
 
•most strategic items revert to their stored post-end-date values
•Phased Out and Obsolete items revert to 0 / 0
 
The reversion is also logged for traceability.
 
Special Behavior by Stock Type
 
New Product Launch
 
For New Product Launch, the module automatically sets:
 
•Stock Start Date
•Stock End Date to six months after the start date
 
This supports a predefined launch stockholding period.
 
Seasonal, Promotion, Obsolete, and Phased Out
 
For these stock types:
 
•customer details are cleared
•estimated monthly consumption is reset
•target min/max values are expected to remain 0 / 0
 
For Obsolete and Phased Out, this helps reduce the risk of accidental reordering.
 
Validation Rules
 
The module includes several controls to protect data quality:
 
•Stock Start Date cannot be in the past
•New Min cannot be greater than New Max
•for Seasonal, Promotion, Obsolete, and Phased Out, target min/max must remain zero
 
These validations help ensure strategic stock is managed consistently and safely.
 
Strategic Stock Log
 
 
Every important strategic stock change will be recorded in the Strategic Stock Log.
 
This log provides historical visibility of:
 
•strategic stock type
•request information
•target min/max values
•original values
•stockholding period
•costs
•reversion activity
 
This gives users an audit trail for strategic inventory decisions.
 
 
Recommendation
 
•Use Strategic Stock Management only for exception-based stock decisions.
•Do not use it as a substitute for normal replenishment planning.
•Record a clear request reason for auditability.
•Maintain realistic end dates and review periods.
•Use customer association fields only when the stock is linked to a specific customer requirement.
•Review phased-out and obsolete items carefully to avoid unnecessary stockholding.
•Check the Strategic Stock Log regularly if multiple users maintain these records.

The Strategic Stock Management tab gives users a controlled way to manage products that require temporary, special, or business-critical stockholding treatment.
 
It helps ensure that exceptional stock decisions are:
 
•documented
•traceable
•linked to reorder rules
•reviewed over time
•automatically reverted when no longer required
 
Strategic Stock Management Form
 
 
1. Classification
 
This section identifies the reason the product is being treated as strategic or exceptional stock.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•Strategic Stock Type
•Requested By
•Request Date
•Request Reason
 
Strategic Stock Type
 
This is the main control field in the tab.
 
Available types include:
 
•Strategically Important
•Customer Specific Request
•Production Material
•MRO Parts
•Seasonal
•Promotion
•Product Lifecycle Management – New Product Launch
•Product Lifecycle Management – Phased Out
•Product Lifecycle Management – Obsolete
•Other
 
This field determines how the product is treated in the strategic stock process.
 
Requested By
 
Records the employee responsible for initiating the strategic stock request.
 
Request Date
 
Stores the date the request was created.
 
Request Reason
 
Allows users to document the reason for holding or managing this product as strategic stock.
 
 
 
2. Customer Association
 
This section is used when the stockholding requirement is linked to a customer.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•Customer Account
•Customer Contact
 
Use this area for customer-specific stock requests or where stock is being held in support of a known account or contact.
 
 
 
3. Consumption Estimate
 
 
This section includes:
 
•Estimated Monthly Consumption
 
This value allows the user to enter an expected monthly usage figure for the product in the context of the strategic stock decision.
 
 
 
4. Stock Holding Period
 
This section controls the time period for which the strategic stock arrangement should remain active.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•Stock Start Date
•Stock End Date
•Review Every (Months)
•Last Review
•Next Review
 
Stock Start Date
 
The date from which the strategic stock period begins.
 
Stock End Date
 
The planned end date for the strategic stock arrangement.
 
Review Every (Months)
 
Defines how often the stock strategy should be reviewed.
 
Last Review
 
Records the last review date.
 
Next Review
 
This field is calculated automatically based on the review interval and last review date.
 
 
 
5. Reordering Overview
 
This section gives visibility into the product’s current reorder values and stock position.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•Current Min
•Current Max
•Stock On Hand
•related cost values for each
 
This helps users compare the current reorder rule against the proposed strategic stock targets.
 
A Reordering Rules button is also available to open the linked Odoo reordering rule directly.
 
 
 
6. Target Min/Max Quantities
 
This section contains the key fields used to define the temporary strategic stock target.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•New Min
•New Max
 
These values represent the target stock levels to be used during the strategic stock period.
 
When maintained, the module synchronizes these values with the product’s reordering rule.
 
 
 
7. Post End Date Quantities
 
This section displays the quantities that will apply after the strategic stock period ends. The values are read-only and cannot be overridden by the user.
 
 
Fields include:
 
•Post End Date Min
•Post End Date Max
•Last Reverted Date
 
These values allow the system to restore or reset reorder values when the strategic stock period expires and displayed help user review re-ordering rules.
 
 
Post End Date Quantities
Phased Out and Obsolete items always revert to 0 / 0, even if a product previous had non-zero re ordering rule.
